Smith College Transportation Emissions

Curious to know the impact of transportation on our carbon footprint?

By: Catherine Weeks, Glory Divine, Mary Alice Wieland

Introduction

Smith College is part of a growing network of institutions using the SIMAP greenhouse gas tracking system to measure and understand its environmental impact. This dataset provides a longitudinal view of campus emissions from 2004 to the present (with some gaps in earlier years), reported in metric tons of greenhouse gases. This data shows how much carbon the school produces each year. The data is grouped into three categories: Scope 1 (energy made on campus), Scope 2 (energy bought from outside), and Scope 3 (other sources like commuting and travel). Our project focuses on transportation, which is mostly part of Scope 3. This includes how students, faculty, and staff travel to and from campus, as well as work-related trips. Some of this information comes from surveys done through Qualtrics, where people report how they commute and travel. This helps estimate how much pollution comes from transportation. By focusing on transportation, we want to show how everyday travel choices affect Smith College’s carbon emissions. We also want to help people on campus-like planners, professors, and students better understand these impacts and think about ways to reduce them in the future.
